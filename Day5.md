Today, I learned an important lesson about working with AI: prompts and context are not the same thing.
I experimented with the same request in two different situations. First, I asked AI to create a 30-day learning roadmap without providing any background information. The result was useful, but it was generic and could have applied to almost anyone.
Then I tried again, this time sharing details about my role, existing skills, career goals, preferred tools, and learning style. The difference was remarkable. Instead of a generic roadmap, I received a personalized learning journey focused on HR data storytelling, tailored specifically to my needs and aspirations.
My biggest takeaway is this:
Prompts tell AI what to do. Context tells AI how to do it for you.
The quality of the output is not determined only by the question being asked. It is heavily influenced by the information provided around that question. The more relevant context AI has, the more personalized, actionable, and valuable its responses become.
Going forward, I want to spend less time trying to perfect the prompt itself and more time providing meaningful context. Sometimes, the best way to get a better answer is not to ask a better question, but to give AI a better understanding of who you are and what you're trying to achieve.
